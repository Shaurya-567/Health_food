Kale Ceasar Quinao Sauce with Fried Rice

Kale Ceasar Quinao provide a healthy base for Fried rice is a dish of cooked rice that has been stir-fried in a wok or a frying pan and is usually mixed with other ingredients such as eggs, vegetables, seafood, or meat. It is often eaten by itself or as an accompaniment to another dish.

Total Time: 1 hour

Ingredients

  • 1/2 Cup Rice
  • 1/2 Bowl Mashroom
  • 1 Bowl Broccoli
  • 2 thsp Olive Oil
  • 1 Bowl Pea
  • 1 thsp Salt, Red Chilli
  • 2 thsp Soya Sauce
  • Scallions

Preparation

  1. I used brown rice, naturally, and even threw in a handful of greens, which offer even more health bonus points. (The greens are optional.)

    Time :10 minutes

  2. That way, each component will be cooked just right, and the vegetables and rice will get a chance to develop those delicious golden edges.

    Time :15 minutes

  3. This recipe comes together quickly. Before you get started, make sure that all of your ingredients are prepped and within an arm’s reach from the stove. Also have an empty bowl nearby for holding the cooked eggs and veggies. I’m suggesting that you start over medium-high heat, but if at any point you catch a whiff of oil or food burning, reduce the heat to medium.

    Time :10 minutes

  4. Warm a large cast iron or stainless steel skillet over medium-high heat until a few drops of water evaporate within a couple of seconds. Immediately add 1 ½ teaspoons of oil and swirl the pan to coat the bottom. Add the scrambled eggs and swirl the pan so they cover the bottom. Cook until they are just lightly set, flipping or stirring along the way. Transfer the eggs to a bowl and wipe out the pan with a heat-proof spatula.

    Time :20 minutes

  5. Divide into bowls and serve immediately. I usually serve mine with chili-garlic sauce or sriracha on the side. Leftovers store well in the refrigerator, covered, for 3 to 4 days (if you used purple cabbage, it might stain your scrambled eggs a funny blue color, but it’s fine to eat).

    Time :5 minutes
